Perception of Emotions in Knocking Sounds: an Evaluation Studymore_vert

DM2350 @ KTH

This is a link
  • info
  • people
  • attachment
    • close
Description

In this study, we create a data set of knocking actions performed with different emotional intentions. We then verify, through a listening test, whether these emotional intentions are perceived through listening to sound alone. Finally, we perform an acoustic analysis of the experimental data set to identify whether emotion-specific acoustic patterns emerge. The results show that emotional intentions are correctly perceived for some emotions. Additionally, the emerging emotion-specific acoustic patterns confirm, at least in part, findings from previous research in speech and music performance.

Collaborators

Alessandro Iop, Malcolm Houel, Alfred Berg, Abhilash Arun, Sandra Pauletto, Adrián Barahona-Ríos.
